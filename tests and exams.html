Examination Rules And Conducts

  • Listen carefully to the invigilator’s instructions.
  • Read all questions thoroughly before answering.
  • Raise your hand if you need help or want to ask a question.
  • Do not talk, whisper, or share materials with anyone.
  • No cheating or copying—any form of malpractice will lead to disqualification.
  • Use your time wisely: attempt the questions you know first.
  • Keep your answer sheet neat and legible.
Dear Students, Exams are not enemies; they are simply opportunities to show what you have learned. But success does not come by wishing or daydreaming—it comes through consistent study, discipline, and hard work. Laziness may feel easy in the moment, but it steals your future rewards. Remember: the effort you put in today determines the results you celebrate tomorrow. Avoid procrastination. Don’t wait until the last minute to open your books. Instead, create a study plan, follow it faithfully, and take each subject seriously. Excellence is built step by step. Every page you read, every exercise you solve, every note you revise is an investment in your own greatness. Also, avoid exam malpractice. Cheating may look like a shortcut, but it always leads to shame and regret. True success is not just about marks—it is about character, honesty, and integrity. When you pass through your exams with sincerity and diligence, you can hold your head high and be proud of yourself. So, rise above laziness. Be focused, be disciplined, and believe in the power of your own effort. Remember: winners are not those who never fail, but those who never quit. Work hard, pray for strength, and trust that your preparation will pay off. The future belongs to those who prepare for it today—make sure you are one of them!
